Cuscuz de Milho

Description

Cuscuz de Milho is a traditional Northeast Brazilian dish made with cornmeal and a variety of vegetables and meats. It is usually steamed and served in slices.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of cornmeal
  • 1 onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 2 green bell peppers
  • 100g of bacon
  • 100g of sausage
  • 2 cups of water
  • Salt to taste
  • Olive oil

Instructions

  1. Finely chop the onion, garlic, tomatoes, and bell peppers.
  2. Cut the bacon and sausage into small pieces.
  3. In a pan, saut√© the onion and garlic in olive oil until they are soft.
  4. Add the tomatoes, bell peppers, bacon, and sausage to the pan and cook for a few minutes.
  5. In a separate bowl, mix the cornmeal with water and salt to form a moist dough.
  6. Grease a cuscuz mold or a heatproof bowl with olive oil and place a layer of the vegetable and meat mixture on the bottom.
  7. Add a layer of the cornmeal dough on top of the vegetables and meat.
  8. Repeat the layers until all the ingredients are used up.
  9. Cover the mold or bowl with aluminum foil and steam it for 30-40 minutes.
  10. Once the cuscuz is cooked, remove it from the mold or bowl and slice it into portions.
  11. Serve the cuscuz de milho warm or at room temperature.
